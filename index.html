About Me

I am an incoming PhD student in Materials Science and Engineering at Northwestern University in the Voorhees Research Group. I recently graduated from Indian Institute of Technology Madras with a bachelors in Metallurgical and Materials Engineering and an interdisciplinary masters in Computational Engineering. I am interested in studying the mechanical behaviour of materials using computational tools. This research area sits at the intersection of materials science, mechanical engineering, computational engineering and mathematics.

On top of my research, one of my personal goals is life-long learning. I get thrilled when unearthing patterns and connections between seemingly unrelated concepts. I recently found a quote that reflects my viewpoint towards learning:

In an age where it is virtually impossible to stay abreast of technology, it is stimulating to find an analysis concept that enables one to approach an unfamilar field with familiar tools.

The quote is from E. O. Brigham's book on Fast Fourier Transforms. I strongly believe the message can be extrapolated.

Over the past two years, I have been a teaching assistant for three undergraduate courses on scientific computing and materials science. Through these endeavors, I got the opportunity to impart my hard-earned knowledge and make the learning curves of others a little easier. I also gained many insights in the process. Through the experience, I have realised that I have a penchant for teaching, and I would like to keep at it during my graduate studies and beyond.

In my free time, I like tinkering with digital tools that I stumble upon. I also love music, books and writing. I am maintaining a collection of some my favourite tools, utilites, books and authors in this page. I hope you find some food for thought in my corner of the internet. If you share some (or all!) of my interests, be sure to reach out!
